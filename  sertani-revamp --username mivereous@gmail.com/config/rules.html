      Be aware that this server, unlike other servers, follows a corporation known as Saris Industries. Information about them may be found on our forums along with other events and organizations you may wish to be aware of. Our forums also allow for posting of role-play backstories, getting to know other players, and making a name for yourself on the server.

      

      Remember that when communicating with an admin to be polite and constructive. We do want what is best for our server and its players. If you are caught lying to an admin you will be banned. If you feel that you have been banned unjustly or an administrator has abused his or her powers, you do have the right to file a complaint on our forums. All complaints will be reviewed and how you present yourself will be taken into account as we review the situation. Always try to be polite and courteous, even if others are not.




Important Terminology [Excerpt from Baystation wiki]

#TERMS


      IC = In Character, means actions and events which happen in-game. Your character only has knowledge of IC actions and nothing from OOC. Using OOC knowledge is therefore meta-gaming. Things people say in-game are IC (e.g. say "Hello!"). Use of internet slang or smileys (E.g. Lol, rofl, :D, ^.^, etc.) is prohibited in IC and books, however, it is allowed in PDA messages.


      OOC = Out of Character, means the goings on outside the game. The things that you, the player, know, see, etc. The two should never be mixed or knowledge from one being used to influence the actions in the other. If you're angry at someone IC, don't bring it to OOC. OOC can be used to ask questions about game mechanics, but should not contain in-game knowledge. For example "How do I build a wall?" is fine. "halp, urist mcguy is beating me" is not okay.


      Griefing = To cause grief to another player for no reason. Examples of this are beating random players senseless for no reason, setting the singularity free for no reason, destroying the station for no reason, stealing vital items for no reason, etc.


      Meta-gaming = Using information acquired Out Of Character to influence the actions in character. An example is learning who the traitor is from IRC, a friend on another computer, through VoIP or through in-game OOC chat (which is breaking the IC in OOC rule!) and then acting on this information. This also means using knowledge that your character, as an employee of Saris Industries, could not have. 

    

As an example, a call to check the shuttle, modes of play, or knowledge of the Syndicate or Syndicate items workings. Using knowledge that your profession would not have is also meta-gaming. This also includes some specifics mentioned below.


      Power-gaming = Also known as 'playing to win'. If you're trying to complete your objectives, kill the antagonists, or 'win', at all costs and ignore RP, you may be better off somewhere else. This includes having your character unafraid of death or pain, resistant to any torture, ready to battle alone against a crowd of opponents, or, being unarmed, senselessly attacking a gun wielder or hostage taker. This also includes loading up your character with items 'just in case', for example wearing internals 'just in case' there's a hull breach or plasma leak, or wearing a space suit indoors for no reason.


      IC in OOC = Saying IC things (like the game mode) in OOC (e.g. "why the heck is it cult again" in secret). Generally, if someone not playing can tell what's going on using OOC, it's IC in OOC. This is generally not a good thing. Don't do it.


      OOC in IC = Talking OOC in IC. Most of the time, this is just bad (e.g "why is some of the game text red"). If you really, REALLY, have to only say something to those around you, you can use ((brackets)) to signify OOC. This is not standard practice however, so don't use it if you can possibly use OOC. Exceptions are made for spelling corrections. Aside from that, there is almost nothing you can say in this fashion that is not better off said OOC.


      Multi-keying = Using multiple BYOND keys/usernames. This is generally not needed ever for legitimate uses and so is a bannable offence.


      Meta-communication = Using IRC, Skype, email, AOL Instant Messenger, a telephone, smoke signals, or any other form of communication between you and another player about IC events in the current round. This is basically the only way to "cheat" in this game, and it's generally an extremely crappy thing to do under all circumstances. Don't do it.


      ERP= Erotic or Sexual Role-play. Does not include such things hugging or holding hands. Does include things that could be considered sexually explicit or inappropriate. If two players wish to participate in ERP they must each contact an administrator for permission. If one wishes to RP a rape scenario, an admin must be notified so as to receive OOC consent from the victim party. Only once consent has been verified will the rape scenario be allowed.


Be sure to have read and understood A Crash Course In Roleplaying.




In Character Rules

#ICRULES


1. Be sure to maintain a consistent character with specific skills and weaknesses. (A character creation skill level of around 15-21 would be acceptable.) Higher skill levels are acceptable for heads of staff or players with written backstories justifying the skill level. If playing a non-head with a strangely high skill set be prepared to provide a written backstory to the admins with the skill set justification.


2. Be sure to follow the role-play guidelines of any non-human species you choose to play unless you are able to provide written backstory justification to the Admins.(Guidelines may be found on the Baystation 12 wiki)


3. Remember that knowledge of the Syndicate and other organizations is typically restricted to the captain and head of security, and even their knowledge is limited. Characters who are not working these two jobs should not have knowledge of the Syndicate unless able to provide a written backstory justification.


4. Racism (not to be confused with specism) will not be tolerated. Do not partake in character, in admin help, on our forum, or out of character. Specism is the prejudice against members of other species on the station. This should be approached with moderation.


5. Almost all forms of crime are permitted in character, but all should receive special role play attention. If a player feels that you poorly role-played your crime you may be reported to an admin for investigation.


6. Avoid forms of slang or 'text talk" such as: Lol, rofl, jk, or :) in game. Also avoid slang for referring to a round antagonist such as: Rev, syndie, or traitor.


7. Attempt to give your character a name that makes sense depending on how you intend to play it.


8. Be sure to understand the effects of clone memory disorder. [Excerpt from Baystation wiki]


There are a few basic rules that apply to most patients, all symptoms of Clone Memory Disorder.


It is caused by the unexpected circumstances after awakening in cryo (if cloned from a backup), or the traumatizing experience of death which is much worse, (if cloned from the corpse).


These are the usual symptoms.


-They will be disoriented after being cloned.


-They will sometimes feel strange urges or fears when confronted with anything that can be directly related to their death. (Only if cloned from the corpse*). 

*See also Symptoms of Trauma for how people in reality would possibly react (read this if you are unsure how to RP Clone Memory Disorder for when you get cloned from a corpse).


9. Avoid killing without a good role-play reason. If planning a murder contact an administrator and be able to justify your intentions.


10. Killing in self-defense is acceptable, but all steps must be taken to escape, call for help and delay, or disarm before committing murder in self-defense.


11. Speak primarily English on the space station and in OOC. Other languages are allowed but be prepared to provide a typed backstory as justification.


12. We follow standard Space Law as displayed on the Baystation wiki.




Antagonist Guidelines

#ANTAG


      When playing as an antagonist, always take into account your goals. All operatives will have different goals depending on their objectives. Some will require stealth. Some will require diplomacy, bribery, and strategy.


1. Avoid killing large numbers of people. We understand that it may be necessary for people to get hurt as you strive to complete your objectives, but you should try to keep the death toll as low as possible.


2. Assume that stealth is always an objective.


3. Role-play according to the morals of the faction you are a part of.


4. Attempt to role-play direct assassinations as much as possible.


5. Torture objectives should receive special role-play attention.


If you do not think that you are able to perform well as an antagonist, speak to an admin.




Special Roles

#SPEC


      Roles such as AI, cyborgs, and Heads of Staff also have important guidelines and expectations.


1. AI's are expected to be computers. They do not have emotions. They will often not speak without good reason or unless spoken too. A malfunctioning AI or traitor AI should still follow these guidelines when completing their objectives.


2. Cyborgs follow an AI's orders without question. They do not have their own emotions, opinions, or objectives.


3. PAI's are excluded from the AI guidelines. They are allowed to have emotions and opinions but must follow their directives.


4. Heads of Staff should delegate their duties as much as possible. Unless short of competent staff, a head should always have their subordinates perform the department’s tasks. This is not to say that the Head should not assist, but they should be less involved than the actual workers.


5. Heads are also expected to keep up to date on records and paperwork. Be sure to apply for permits on construction. Update medical and security records. Keep logs of research. Security should file event reports. Copies of Security and Medical records should be printed in the event of archive corruption. Quartermasters should keep shipping records, Head of Personnels should keep access change and job changes recorded and archived. Paperwork templates can be found here.




      That is about it. If you have any questions feel free to ask on admin help or on the forum. Admin help can be accessed from the admin tab in the top right hand corner of your screen. So can the forum and the wiki. Enjoy your time on Sertani Station 6.
